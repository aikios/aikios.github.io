Here I am writing this blog. It is 8:27 PM on Friday night, meaning that I have about 3 and a half hours to write this thing. But I don’t know what to write about, so I will write about blogging. Blogging is somewhat fun, otherwise, why would I do it? The answer, for those who don’t feel like thinking, is that because my grade depends on it. Does this mean that I don’t enjoy blogging? I’ll leave that for you to ponder.

Today, I got rejected from the University of Chicago. Why did I get rejected? I don’t know. Maybe its not my place to know. I catch myself thinking that it is because I did not try that hard on the application. I only wrote one draft of each essay, and I wrote both essays in the span of approximately 3 hours one evening. I think about this, and realize that maybe this is just me coping, trying to soften the blow of the rejection. Then I think: I was not ever enthusiastic about uChicago, not at the start, and not now. But then I think: why did I apply to a college that I was not enthusiastic about?

The other day, I was really enthusiastic about wanting to play Mario Kart. So I bought a copy. Today, I put it in the Nintendo Switch, and I played two or three Grand Prixs. It was then that I realized that Mario Kart is not as fun when you are playing it alone. Plus, every 5 seconds I would think about this blog, which was not being written while I played Mario Kart. So I stopped playing Mario Kart, and started the hourlong dithering process which is a prerequisite to me writing a blog.

As I write this blog, I am listening to Michael Jackson. Is it possible for a human to lean like Michael Jackson does in the music video for “Smooth Criminal”? Ostensibly, the answer is yes: Michael Jackson make a 45-degree angle with his body and the floor, so humans, by extension, should be able to perform this feat too. This is strangely (actually it is not very strange at all) not true: in the video, MJ wore special shoes, which one would imagine were fastened in some manner to the floor. Humans cannot perform a 45 degree lean -- I invite you with all my heart to prove me wrong --- maybe human advancement is held behind a locked door, the key to which is a human body making a 45 degree angle.

Was Annie OK, having been accosted by a polished felon? MJ does not provide us with a conclusive answer, instead choosing to repeat the question.

Now another song, of the “lo-fi” variety, comes on. I am listening to it, but lets say I didn’t. Let’s say I skipped it, and the one after that, and the one after that, and the one after that, and the one after that, and the one after that (ad nauseam). I think about a song which I would like to listen to, but I cannot think of any specific song. Eventually I just close out YouTube because suddenly, I do not want to listen to music. I am reminded of a book we read in freshman year, or maybe it was sophomore year, something about science but not: in it, a scenario is posed, in which a customer walks past a display of jams. A (presumably scientific) study is conducted, and customers are more likely to purchase a jar of jam when they are given a limited choice, as opposed to a wide variety of jams to peruse. People can be paralyzed by something as simple as a (tasteful? you decide) selection of jams.

When I’m in the car, I like to listen to the radio. I never feel like turning the radio off, only switching to another station maybe when the ads come on (this is if I feel like exerting the effort to seek the other station (this was before I discovered how to set preset stations in my (not really mine) car)). Remembering reading about an online radio station, I decide to turn that on, and I feel much more satisfied. I think there is something about having other people decide for you that just feels a lot more comfortable (the reason is that you don’t have to feel responsible for what goes wrong).

I wish that someone would just tell me which college I am going to, and it could be decided, and I could rest easy, not having to worry about decisions and such. But this cannot be the way that it works. Why not? The reason is not important. I now notice that the blog has come in a circle. I also notice that I wrote a significant amount more than what I was aiming to write: the vague number of words that seems like just right for a blog post: somewhere around a page, a little shorter than an essay, but definitely not a single paragraph. We are beyond that kind of measure, and I am reminded of an em dash—of which I recently learned the name. But since the blog has come in a circle, it is not so much an em dash as a ○ (this character does not have a name that I am aware of, so we will just refer to it as the circle). I think now that what I have written is a satisfactory blog: it is of length, and it expresses a complete (my interpretation, maybe yours as well) narrative: we have presented a series of unrelated things, and tied most of them back together in an interpretation of a (relatively (but not really)) significant event in my life.

I am going to go do something else now.
